About Us

Drama House’s goal is to create and promote a vibrant, educational, and student driven performing arts community on campus. Our members endeavor to do this by producing original workshops and lectures, providing a financially feasible rehearsal and performance space for student events, and building a home for artistically minded students to collaborate.

Since its inception in 1971, Drama House has stood for our principles of creativity, artistic collaboration, and gender and sexuality inclusion. Regardless of academic pursuit, many of its members remain passionately dedicated to theatre. They also posses a broad array of interests including music, film, dance, literature, and fine arts. Drama House produces and hosts events which focus on encouraging a strong campus and local theatre community. When not in use for our own events, the space is made available to other groups who share our mission, many of which our residents are proudly part of.

Located at 595 Fraternity Road.

For more info, see our House Constitution.

Hosted Events

Drama House's mission is to foster the performing arts on our campus. Therefore our space is used by numerous performance groups. The Opposite of People, a student theatre company founded by enthusiastic Drama House members, performs two mainstage shows every semester. In Between the Lines, the hilarious and spontaneous improvisational troupe, hosts performances, rehearsals, and Open Mondays. UR Concerts hosts ROC Thursdays, a frequently occurring event featuring student performers. Other groups with an active presence are No Jackets Required, the Music Interest Floor, and WRUR.

The Miraculous Life of Nicholas Potter

Once upon a time, in a small town in France, a boy named Nick Jonas was born. He was the perfect child, until one day, his sister’s child was close to death and Nick stole a loaf of bread for his starving family. Of course, he was arrested and sent to jail.

At the beginning of his tenth year, the jail became overcrowded and it was announced that some of the convicts would have to travel across the sea to America. Nick stood up and said “I volunteer!” Nick was loaded onto a large ship along with many civilians and zoo animals, and they set off for America. On the fourth day of the journey, while the captain wasn’t looking, the ship hit an iceberg! Somehow, Potter ended up in a lifeboat with a Bengal tiger. This is how he made the voyage to America, with only his philosophical thoughts and the tiger for company.

Upon reaching America, Nick was put into an orphan boys’ home. The tiger was deported because there are no cats in America. It was a hard-knock life for little orphan Nick. At meal times, he always asked if he could have some more, but he was always laughed away. After a year, he was adopted by an old couple who asked him to call them Auntie Em and Count Olaf. This is how Nick Jonas became Nick Potter, later known simply as Potter.

The couple already had a son named Taylor who attended Hogwarts at the time and would grow up to run his own human farm. They later gained a sister named Christina, who became a famous pop star. Nick’s brother Taylor had a best friend named Danica who became a close friend to Nick and the rest of the family as well. She was like the perfect child they never had. The Potters did have one more child: the aptly named Sir Not Appearing in This Biography.

Nick and a friend were playing in the creek one day when his friend discovered a small golden ring. They fought over who should keep the ring, and Nick came out victorious. The whereabouts of his friend are still unknown.

When Nick was about sixteen, he was on a field trip to a science lab and got separated from his class when using his new ring to disappear. He ended up being bitten by a radioactive spider that was on display. On his way home after the field trip, Nick witnessed his uncle being shot by a mugger. Nick approached the mugger and said “My name is Nick Potter. You killed my father. Prepare to die,” and then unleashed his new spidey powers on the man. The man did not actually die, but that was how Nick discovered he had the ability to fight crime

Besides being a superhero, and of course, stage managing, Nick likes to spend his time playing barnyard pool, helping out on his brother’s farm, playing in Sgt. Pepper’s Lonely Hearts Club Band, drinking water, and being awesome. Around the holidays, Nick is very generous. He likes to leave many secret gifts, such as coins in a pair of shoes. On Wednesdays, he wears pink. Many people don’t realize that Nick is actually a well-published author; his works include The Bro Code and The Playbook (both available at your local Barnes and Noble). He is also known for his work on the St. Petersburg Pardox.

Monster Mosh

October 31st 10 PM - 2 AM

Halloween is our annual Autumn open party! Entertainment features student bands who have the opportunity for a paid gig on campus. Everyone comes in costume and ready to have a great time. There is always a costume party taken most seriously by those who make their costumes (and many of the ones for performances).

Bacchanalia

April 9th 10 PM - 2 AM

Bacchanalia is our annual Spring open party! Partiers are expected to sport their best bed-sheet-toga. Like Halloween we feature student bands throughout the night. In the spirit of Bacchus and the Romans, wine flows all night!

Thanksgiving

November 22nd 5 PM - 7 PM

Kicked off with a toast from the Artistic Director of the International Theatre Program, guests enjoy a plentiful Thanksgiving dinner with the entire River Campus theatre community. Guests are professors, the cast and crew of the current productions, members of on campus theatre clubs come join us for a night of food and friends. Each drama house member makes a dish to bring the entire meal together.

Toscars

May 1st 12 PM - 2 PM

With such a vibrant on campus theatre community, Drama House applauds all of the hardworking thespians of River Campus. At the end of every year, we hold an awards ceremony, Toscars, to do just that. Members of students and academic groups alike are recognized for talent, effort, and their gaffes. Awards include best male/female/androgynous role, stage manager of the year, student director of the year, best off stage diva, and the like. Some awards presented by the International Theatre Program include scholarships.

Fireside Chats

TBA

Every semester, we host a fireside chat. A professor or other distinguished speaker will come in to talk about one of their areas of expertise in a relaxed discussion setting. We will frequently have a co-sponsor for our fireside chats where the discussion is then something relevant to both of the groups’ interests. Previous topics include: Audio Engineering with Prof Mark Bocko (Department of Physics, University of Rochester), Theatricality in the Bible with Prof. Mark Brummitt (Colgate Rochester Crozer Divinity School), Theatrical Lighting with Prof. Gordon Rice (International Theatre Program, University of Rochester), and many others.

Drama House hosts many other events that are organized by our members. These events are members’ opportunity to make new and exciting contributions to campus life with the support the entire house.
